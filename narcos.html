About Narcos:

"Narcos" is an American crime drama television series that follows the story of Colombian narcoterrorist and drug lord Pablo Escobar, leader of the Medell√≠n Cartel, who amassed immense wealth through the production and distribution of cocaine. Created and produced by Chris Brancato, Carlo Bernard, and Doug Miro, the series is set and filmed in Colombia, with the first two seasons focusing on Escobar's criminal empire and interactions with various entities, including drug lords and DEA agents.

Netflix renewed "Narcos" for a third and fourth season on September 6, 2016. Season 3, exploring the aftermath of Escobar's downfall, premiered on September 1, 2017. However, the series took a different turn after Season 3. On July 18, 2018, it was announced that Season 4 would reset as the first season of a new series titled "Narcos: Mexico." Released on November 16, 2018, "Narcos: Mexico" unfolds in the 1980s and delves into the rise of drug cartels in Mexico.
