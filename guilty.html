About Guilty:

"Guilty" is a 2020 Indian Hindi-language thriller drama film directed by Ruchi Narain and written by Ruchi Narain, Kanika Dhillon, and Atika Chohan. Starring Kiara Advani, the film addresses the sensitive topic of sexual assault during the #MeToo era. Nanki Dutta, a songwriter for her boyfriend Vijay "VJ" Pratap Singh's band, faces a moral dilemma when a new student, Tanu Kumar, accuses VJ of sexual assault as part of the #MeToo movement.

In an attempt to help VJ, Nanki consults Danish Ali Baig, the lawyer handling VJ's case. The film unfolds with Nanki experiencing panic attacks and hallucinations while dealing with the aftermath of the accusations. Despite Danish winning the case and VJ's release, Nanki remains suspicious due to inconsistencies in the story. During a campus festival, Tanu reveals the truth about the fateful night, exposing VJ as a perpetrator. Nanki realizes that Tanu's #MeToo tweet was true, leading to VJ's arrest by local authorities. Encouraged by Tanu, Nanki finds the courage to share her own experience of sexual abuse. "Guilty" explores themes of trust, manipulation, and the complexities of addressing sexual assault within the context of the #MeToo movement.
