About Altered Carbon:

"Altered Carbon" is an American cyberpunk television series created by Laeta Kalogridis, based on the 2002 novel of the same name by Richard K. Morgan. Set in a future where consciousness can be transferred between bodies, the story follows Takeshi Kovacs, a former soldier turned investigator, who is released from prison to solve a murder. The first season, comprising ten episodes, premiered on Netflix on February 2, 2018.


In this future, memories and consciousness are recorded onto disk-shaped devices called cortical stacks, implanted in the vertebrae at the back of the neck. These stacks, of alien design, are mass-produced but can only be made from the material on Harlan's World. Bodies, whether human or synthetic, are referred to as "sleeves," and stacks can be transferred to new bodies after death. However, if a stack is destroyed with no backup, the person can be permanently killed. The wealthiest individuals, known as "Meths," have the ability to change bodies through clones and remote storage of their consciousness in satellites, allowing them to avoid death from old age before being resleeved.
