About Snow Piercer:

"Snowpiercer" is an American post-apocalyptic dystopian thriller television series that aired on TNT from May 17, 2020, until March 28, 2022. The show is based on both the 2013 film of the same name, directed by Bong Joon-ho, and the 1982 French graphic novel "Le Transperceneige" by Jacques Lob, Benjamin Legrand, and Jean-Marc Rochette, which inspired the film. As a reboot of the film's continuity, the series unfolds on the Snowpiercer, an enormous, perpetually moving train circling the globe, housing the remnants of humanity seven years after the world transforms into a frozen wasteland. The narrative delves into themes of class warfare, social injustice, and the politics of survival.


The main cast includes Jennifer Connelly, Daveed Diggs, Mickey Sumner, Alison Wright, Iddo Goldberg, Susan Park, Katie McGuinness, Sam Otto, Sheila Vand, Mike O'Malley, Annalise Basso, Jaylin Fletcher, Lena Hall, and Roberto Urbina. Steven Ogg, Rowan Blanchard, and Sean Bean joined the main cast in the second season, while Chelsea Harris and Archie Panjabi became part of the main cast in the third season. The series faced significant production challenges and delays during its development at TNT, primarily due to creative differences between producers and the network. Originally set to air on TBS, it was later decided to remain on TNT.It was announced that the fourth season would not air on TNT and was in the process of moving elsewhere.
