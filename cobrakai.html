About Cobra Kai:

"Cobra Kai" is an American martial arts comedy-drama television series and a sequel to the original "The Karate Kid" films created by Robert Mark Kamen. Developed by Josh Heald, Jon Hurwitz, and Hayden Schlossberg, the series is distributed by Sony Pictures Television. Initially released on YouTube Red/Premium for the first two seasons, it later moved to Netflix starting with the third season. The show features Ralph Macchio and William Zabka reprising their roles as Daniel LaRusso and Johnny Lawrence, respectively, from the 1984 film "The Karate Kid" and its sequels.


"Cobra Kai" offers a fresh perspective on the "Miyagi-verse" narrative, presenting events from Johnny's viewpoint. It begins with his decision to reopen the Cobra Kai karate dojo, reigniting his old rivalry with Daniel LaRusso. The series spans from 2017 to 2019 and also stars Courtney Henggeler, Xolo Maridueña, Tanner Buchanan, Mary Mouser, Jacob Bertrand, Gianni DeCenzo, Peyton List, Vanessa Rubio, and Dallas Dupree Young. Martin Kove and Thomas Ian Griffith reprise their roles from the original films.
