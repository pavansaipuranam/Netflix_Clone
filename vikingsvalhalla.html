About Vikings Valhalla:

"Vikings: Valhalla," often referred to simply as "Valhalla," is a historical drama television series created by Jeb Stuart for Netflix. Positioned as a sequel to the popular series "Vikings," the first season, comprising eight episodes, premiered on February 25, 2022. The show is set more than 100 years after the events depicted in "Vikings" and is centered in England, where tensions between the descendants of the Vikings (referred to as Norse or "Danes") and the native English escalate to a violent climax. Within the Norse community, conflicts arise over differing Christian and pagan beliefs.


In November 2019, Netflix announced a substantial 24-episode order for the series. Following its debut season, "Vikings: Valhalla" was officially renewed for a second and third season in March 2022. The second season premiered on January 12, 2023. The narrative is expected to unfold over key historical events, beginning with the St. Brice's Day massacre in 1002 and concluding with the Battle of Stamford Bridge in 1066, marking the end of the Viking Age. The third and final season is anticipated to premiere in 2024. The series has continued the legacy of "Vikings" and explores the cultural clashes and transformations during this tumultuous historical period.
